Maintenance SLAs

Clear service levels for refrigeration and chilled system maintenance, emergency support, and preventative care.

Service Levels Designed for Reliability

Our Service Level Agreements (SLAs) define expectations, response targets, and the scope of support for commercial cooling systems.

1. Scope of Coverage

ColdMan Refrigeration SLAs cover the maintenance, repair, and emergency support of commercial cold rooms, industrial chillers, vessel freezers, HVAC systems, and other documented refrigeration assets.

Coverage includes routine preventative maintenance, inspection of refrigerant circuits, electrical diagnostics, compressor performance checks, and system calibration.

2. Response Times

  • Emergency Response: 45 minutes from notification to dispatch for critical failures affecting food safety, product integrity, or continuous production.
  • Urgent Service: 4 business hours for high-priority faults that threaten system performance but do not immediately endanger storage conditions.
  • Standard Maintenance: 24 to 48 business hours for scheduled inspections, routine repairs, and non-critical maintenance work.

3. Preventative Maintenance

Preventative maintenance visits are scheduled quarterly or monthly, depending on asset criticality. Each visit includes:

  • Visual and thermal inspection of compressors, condensers, evaporators, and piping.
  • Refrigerant charge validation and leak detection.
  • Electrical safety checks, control system verification, and alarm testing.
  • Cleaning of heat exchange surfaces, fans, filters, and condensate removal systems.

4. Performance Targets

Our core operating targets are engineered to preserve uptime and minimize operational risk.

  • System Availability: 99.9% uptime for covered refrigeration and chiller systems where the scope is fully defined.
  • First-Time Fix: 85% first-time fix rate on maintenance visits when parts and system history are available.
  • Service Accuracy: Complete and accurate service reports delivered within 24 hours of each visit.

5. Client Responsibilities

To maintain SLA validity, the client must:

  • Provide timely access to refrigeration equipment, control rooms, and facility areas.
  • Supply accurate system documentation, service logs, and maintenance history.
  • Report faults promptly through our official dispatch channels.
  • Acknowledge that delays caused by restricted access, unavailable parts, or third-party interventions may affect response targets.

6. Exclusions and Limitations

SLA coverage does not include:

  • Damage caused by force majeure, intentional misuse, vandalism, or unauthorized modifications.
  • Failures due to pre-existing defects in equipment supplied by third parties outside the agreed scope.
  • Cosmetic repairs, structural building work, or supply chain delays for custom replacement components.
  • Any work not explicitly documented within the agreed SLA schedule or maintenance plan.

7. Reporting and Documentation

Every service visit includes a technical report that documents:

  • The condition of the system, findings, and recommended corrective actions.
  • Work completed, parts installed, and any follow-up actions required.
  • Measured temperatures, pressures, and operational checks relevant to system performance.

Reports are delivered electronically to the client within one business day of completion.

8. Revision and Review

SLAs are reviewed annually or whenever a client changes their operational requirements. Revisions can be made by mutual agreement to ensure the agreement remains aligned with evolving business needs.
